### Print Method: What's the Difference?

- **Screen printing** — Best for solid colors and large runs. Pantone-accurate, durable, cost-effective at scale.
- **Heat transfer** — Ideal for photographic or gradient artwork. Slightly higher per-unit cost, no plate fee.
- **Digital printing** — Maximum design freedom, no minimum color restriction. Best for small runs or complex artwork.

## Step 2 — Request a Quote

_Timeline: Day 2–3 | Response within 24 hours_

A good quote should answer six questions. If a supplier's quote doesn't include all six, ask before proceeding.

1. **Unit price** at your target quantity
2. **MOQ** — minimum order quantity for this specific SKU
3. **Plate/setup fee** — one-time cost per color per screen (waived in certain conditions — see below)
4. **Sample fee** — most factories charge for physical samples; we offer one free sample round per inquiry
5. **Lead time** — from order confirmation to goods ready at factory
6. **Payment terms** — T/T, LC, or other arrangements

## Step 3 — Sampling and Design Approval

_Timeline: Day 3–12 (digital proof: Day 3–5 / physical sample: Day 7–12)_

This is the most important stage in protecting your order. Rushing it is the single most common cause of bulk-order disappointments.

### Two Rounds of Confirmation

**Round 1 — Digital Proof (PDF/AI)** We send a scaled, print-ready digital mockup showing:

- Exact color separations with Pantone references
- Dimensions and position of all print elements
- Handle attachment points and stitching detail
- Front, back, and gusset views

This is not a screen preview — it is the production file. Approve it only when every element is correct.

**Round 2 — Physical Sample** A factory-produced sample is made using the approved digital proof. This is your chance to check:

- Color accuracy vs. your Pantone specification (tolerance: ±5 Delta E)
- Handle strength and stitching quality
- Fabric hand-feel and weight
- Actual dimensions under load

<!-- 📸 IMAGE: Side-by-side of a printed paper digital proof and the resulting physical sample bag on a light surface. -->

### Color Accuracy: Our Commitment

> All production colors are matched to **Pantone Solid Coated** or our in-house calibrated color library. Screen display colors are **not** used as reference. If your approved sample and bulk order have a color difference exceeding ±5 Delta E, we will re-run the affected units at no charge.

> **Note on turnaround:** Physical samples ship within 5–7 business days of digital proof approval. International sample shipping via DHL Express is complimentary for first-time orders.

## Step 4 — Place Your Order and Confirm Production

_Timeline: Day 10–12_

Once you've signed off on the physical sample, production begins. Here's what happens at this stage:

- **Order confirmation** sent within 24 hours with itemized invoice
- **Deposit invoice** issued (typically 30% T/T in advance, balance before shipment)
- **Production slot assigned** — your dedicated account manager sends a written production schedule with milestone dates
- **Factory order number issued** — use this to track your order at any point

> **What if I need to make a change after placing the order?** Minor changes (quantity adjustment ±10%, delivery address) are possible within 48 hours of order confirmation. Design or specification changes after production has started may incur a retooling fee. Always confirm in writing.

_Timeline: Day 10–28 (depending on quantity)_

Our production spans **9 manufacturing bases** across China, with a combined annual output of **500 million units**. All production facilities hold:

### Our 3-Stage QC Process

<!-- 📸 IMAGE: Triptych of three QC images — (1) raw material inspection with calipers, (2) in-line machine operator checking print registration, (3) final inspector at packing table with AQL board visible. Caption: "Quality control at every stage: raw material, in-line, and pre-shipment." -->

**Stage 1 — Incoming Material Inspection** Every roll of fabric is tested for GSM (weight), tensile strength, and colorfastness before entering production. Substandard materials are rejected and returned to the supplier.

**Stage 2 — In-Line Production Checks** Our QC team conducts random checks every 2 hours during printing and assembly. Print registration, stitch count, and handle attachment strength are checked against the signed sample.

**Stage 3 — Pre-Shipment AQL Inspection** Before packing, all finished goods undergo AQL 2.5 sampling inspection. A full quality inspection report (with photos) is issued with every shipment.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can you accept orders below your standard MOQ?** Yes, in most cases. Small-batch production is available for select product lines, sometimes as low as 100 pcs. Minimum order surcharges may apply. Send us your quantity and we'll advise on the most cost-effective route.

**How do you handle color accuracy across large runs?** We use Pantone Solid Coated as the production standard — not screen color. Our calibrated inkroom is tested daily. Bulk order color tolerance is ±5 Delta E vs. the signed physical sample.

**What happens if my delivery is delayed?** We issue a written delay notice minimum 10 days before the original delivery date, with two alternative solutions (partial shipment, expedited shipping upgrade). Our contracts include a delivery guarantee clause.
