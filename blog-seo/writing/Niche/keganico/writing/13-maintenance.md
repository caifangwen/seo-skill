## 示例 (Fixed Blade Knife)

```markdown
## Maintenance and Care

### Cleaning

**After Each Use:**
1. Wipe the blade with a soft, dry cloth
2. Remove any moisture, sap, or debris
3. For sticky residue, use warm soapy water

**Deep Cleaning (Monthly):**
1. Wash with mild soap and warm water
2. Use a soft brush for hard-to-reach areas
3. Dry thoroughly immediately after washing
4. Apply a light coat of oil to the blade

### Sharpening

**When to Sharpen:**
- When the knife struggles to cut paper
- When you feel increased resistance during cutting
- As part of regular maintenance (every 3-6 months for EDC)

**How to Sharpen:**
1. **Choose your tool**: Whetstone, ceramic rod, or guided system
2. **Set the angle**: Most knives use 15-20 degrees per side
3. **Sharpen**: Push the blade away from you, maintaining consistent angle
4. **Repeat**: 10-15 strokes per side, or until burr forms
5. **Hone**: Use a leather strop for a polished edge
6. **Test**: Cut paper to verify sharpness

### Storage

**Best Practices:**
- Store in a dry place
- Use a sheath to protect the edge
- Avoid leather sheaths for long-term storage (traps moisture)
- Apply a thin coat of oil before extended storage
- Keep away from corrosive materials

### What to Avoid

❌ **Don't**:
- Use as a pry bar or screwdriver
- Cut on hard surfaces (glass, stone, metal)
- Leave wet or dirty
- Store in leather sheath long-term
- Put in dishwasher
- Use on frozen foods

✅ **Do**:
- Cut on wood or plastic cutting boards
- Clean and dry after each use
- Oil the blade periodically
- Check for loose handles
- Professional sharpening when needed
```
