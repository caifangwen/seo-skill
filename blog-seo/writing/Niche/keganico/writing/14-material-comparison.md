## 示例 (14C28N vs D2)

```markdown
## 14C28N vs D2

| Feature | **14C28N** | **D2** | **Winner & Why** |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Toughness Score | 9.0 | 3.5 | **14C28N** – 2.5x tougher than D2 |
| Edge Retention | 3.0 | 5.0 | **D2** – Slight edge |
| Corrosion Resistance | 8.5 | 4.5 | **14C28N** – Far superior |
| Maintenance Need | Minimal | High | **14C28N** – Nearly maintenance-free |
| Ease of Sharpening | Very Easy | Difficult | **14C28N** – Huge time saver |

### Detailed Analysis

**Toughness**

14C28N's 9.0 toughness score is 2.5 times that of D2. This means 14C28N can withstand
significantly more impact without chipping or cracking. Manufacturers can use more
aggressive edge geometry (15-17 degrees) with 14C28N, while D2 typically requires
20+ degrees for strength.

**Corrosion Resistance**

Many D2 users switch to 14C28N primarily due to maintenance burden. D2's 4.5 corrosion
resistance score means it requires regular oiling to prevent rust. A Florida user shared:
"In humid environments, D2 is a nightmare. Even with frequent oiling, rust appears.
After switching to 14C28N, I almost forgot what rust looks like."

**Ease of Sharpening**

14C28N's fine grain structure makes it one of the easiest quality steels to sharpen.
Using standard whetstones, you can restore a razor edge in 5-10 minutes. D2, being a
tool steel with high wear resistance, requires diamond stones and 20-30 minutes of
patient work.

### Recommendation

**Choose 14C28N if**: You live in humid environments, want low-maintenance steel, or
prefer easy sharpening. Ideal for EDC and kitchen knives.

**Choose D2 if**: You prioritize edge retention over corrosion resistance, need a
budget work knife, or don't mind regular maintenance.
```
