# SKILL-10: 历史背景叙述

**用途**: 撰写产品/材料的历史背景
**适用**: 产品指南文章、行业科普文章

---

## 结构模板

```markdown
## History of [产品/技术]

[起源时间] + [起源地点/人群]（1 句）

[发展历程]（1-2 句）

[现代应用/地位]（1 句）
```

---

## 常用句式

```
- "[产品] have been used by [行业] since [时期]."
- "The concept of [技术] was first introduced by [人物/公司] in [年份]."
- "Originally designed for [原始用途], it has evolved to serve [现代应用]."
- "The standardization of [产品] began in [年份] when [组织] published [标准号]."
```

---

## 示例（法兰）

```markdown
## History of Pipe Flanges

Pipe flanges have been used since the early days of industrial piping systems in the 19th century. The need for detachable connections in steam engines and early oil pipelines drove the development of standardized flange designs.

The American Water Works Association (AWWA) published some of the earliest flange standards in the late 1800s. As industries grew, so did the need for uniform specifications. The ASME B16.5 standard, first published in the 1930s, became the benchmark for steel flanges in North America.

Today, flanges are manufactured to meet various international standards including ASME, DIN, JIS, and GOST, enabling global trade and interoperability in industrial projects.
```

---

## 示例（球阀）

```markdown
## History of the Ball Valve

The ball valve was invented in the 1950s as an improvement over traditional plug valves. The design utilized a rotating ball with a bore through the center, providing reliable shutoff with minimal torque.

Initially used in industrial applications, the ball valve quickly gained popularity due to its bubble-tight sealing and quarter-turn operation. The introduction of PTFE seats in the 1960s further enhanced performance and expanded temperature ranges.

Modern ball valves are now available in various configurations including floating ball, trunnion-mounted, and V-port designs, serving industries from oil and gas to pharmaceuticals.
```

## 示例（不锈钢）

```markdown
## History of Stainless Steel in Piping Applications

Stainless steel was discovered in 1913 by Harry Brearley in Sheffield, England, when he noticed that steel with chromium content resisted rusting. This breakthrough transformed multiple industries, including piping systems.

The first commercial applications of stainless steel in pipes and fittings emerged in the 1920s, primarily for food processing and chemical industries. The development of 304 and 316 grades in the 1930s established the foundation for modern stainless steel specifications.

During World War II, stainless steel production scaled rapidly for military applications. Post-war, it became the material of choice for sanitary, corrosive, and high-temperature applications across civilian industries.
```
